Paul Pfleuger, Jr.

Lynne Rees

salmon run
does the river too
have memory?

Yvonne Cabalona

 

remembering him
the worn shell
still pink inside

Lynne Steel

 

moonlight
  on the rocky side
     of an oyster shell

Helen Buckingham

 

outstretched cormorant
the sun rays filter
through my fingers

Paula Fisher

 

dreaming aloud
until the tide
touches our feet

Paul Pfleuger, Jr.

 
 
 

island shrine
the slap of salmon
across the water

Kay Grimnes

 

sea and sky
the sand slipping out
from under my feet

Christopher Patchel

 

alone with my thoughts . . .
the wet sand a mirror
between ebb and flow

Connie Donleycott

 

high tide
jellyfish
moons

George Swede

 

sun along the shore
even the grey cockle shells
surprise me

Lynne Rees
