Joann Klontz

the faint shine
of camellia buds . . .
winter moon

Beverley George

 

not a bride anymore
still the plum
blossoms

Hana Nestieva

 

lambing
the smell of wet sweaters
fills the barn

Bruce Boynton

 

spring downpour . . .
so many words
for rain

Peter Newton

 

cattle lowing . . .
a clod of earth
clings to the fork

Michele L. Harvey

 
 
 

sunny wall
the marks of time
on my skin

Luciano Costa

 

the valley’s browns
still edged with white
tinkle of the beck

John Barlow

 

spring rain
my garden
still in packets

Claudette Russell

 

more rain
I know I know I squawk back
at a jay

Yvonne Cabalona

 

lilacs in bloom
a clerk washes
the town hall windows

Joann Klontz
