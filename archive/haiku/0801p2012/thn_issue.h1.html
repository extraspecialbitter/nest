Lane Parker

winter evening
a light is burning
in the back of the house

Jerry Ball

 

winter doldrums
my landlady enumerates
the perennials

Christopher Patchel

 

winter silence . . .
     another armload
             of junk mail

Ed Markowski

 

spring rain
the crumpled obituary
by my bed

Aurora Antonovic

 

mausoleum —
just passing
through

Yu Chang

 
 
 

from the teahouse
the smell of must
fallen cherry leaves

Margaret Chula

 

fallen leaves —
a line of snails
in the bark fold

John Barlow

 

swollen stream —
my friend talks about
the cancer

Gary Steinberg

 

Rain and wind —
in the circle of mourners
one person smiling

George Swede

 

Monday morning
ant on the coffee cup
going in circles

Lane Parker
