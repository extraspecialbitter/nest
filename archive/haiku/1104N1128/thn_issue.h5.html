Cora Whitmore

ancient fir scar —
a banana slug pulls one eye
inward

Ruth Yarrow

 

blackberry stains
on the white sidewalk
his urgent kiss

Megan Arkenberg

 

late summer talk —
the oil lamp smoke
going in circles

Elizabeth Crocket

 

sunset . . .
the fishing boats closer
to each other

Valeria Simonova-Cecon

 

cracked boulder
an acorn
settles in

Yu Chang

 
 
 

shelling almonds
bits and pieces of
childhood

Speranza Spiratos

 

Lolita on the hammock . . .
       floating back to its branch
                  a butterfly

Scott Mason

 

end of summer
the sting of a thorn
through my garden glove

Joann Klontz

 

essays to grade
a car stirs dust
on the gravel road

LeRoy Gorman

 

cattail seed
floating — the places
I would go

Cora Whitmore
