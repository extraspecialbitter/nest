hot night
the moth fans a breeze
through the screen

William Cullen, Jr.

 

sudden gust–
the book opens to a poem
I like even better

Carolyn Hall

 

approaching storm
the young blackbird struggles
with a worm

John Crook

 

summer rain–
cat draped
over the footstool

Carolyn Thomas

 

summer lethargy . . .
     between faucet drips
the ticking of the kitchen clock

Rick Tarquinio

 
 
 

sound of a bomber
wind is striking
the pulled-down blind

Zoran Doderovic

 

summer begins . . .
   the underground sprinkler head
      raises a rock

Zolo

 

downpour
all the puddles
wash away

Mark Brooks

 
leisurely gossip
our neighbor's dog
snaps at flies
John Stevenson

 

this heat
only the shadows
move

J. D. Heskin
