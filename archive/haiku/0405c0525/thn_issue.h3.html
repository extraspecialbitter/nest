heat lightning–
the first drop of sweat
on her chin

John W. Wisdom

 

echo of a bell . . .
a monk checks his step
over a garden snail

J. B. Leong

 

open window
the neighbor’s parrot answers
my oven timer

Yvonne Hardenbrook

 

dawn
a vee of goslings swims
toward the moon

Francis W. Alexander

 

wipers wiping
slush from the windshield–
radio love song

Alice Frampton

 
 
 

a slug trail–
moonlight
streaks my shoe

Sabine Miller

 

pink clouds!
so hot the morning
the circus arrives

Michael McClintock

 

afternoon bath
the mockingbird echoes
a thrush

Mark Brooks

 
late autumn–
driving toward the sunset
all the way home
Adelaide Shaw

 

fumbling
to unlock the door–
winter stars

Lenard D. Moore
