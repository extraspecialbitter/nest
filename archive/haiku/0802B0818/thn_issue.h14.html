an’ya

Yvonne Cabalona

Carlos Colón

talk of peace
an ovenbird nests
on a rusted trap

Robert Bauer

 

cabin fever
a wandering mastiff
smells each tulip

Ariel Lambert

 

vast blue sky
     we empty
     her closets

Carolyn Hall

 

the greening pastures
one hawk’s circles
answered by another

Lynne Steel

 

almond blossoms
the weight
of raindrops

Yvonne Cabalona

 
 
 

late snow —
in the wire basket
onion sprouts

Beverly Tift

 

early thaw —
a kitten puts his front paws
on the windowsill

Olga Hooper

 

once again
just the two of us . . .
snowdrops in bloom

Connie Donleycott

 

bull’s nose —
a swarm of bees
follows the pollen

an’ya

 

sixth day sick
dandelion seed
in a spider web

Carlos Colón
