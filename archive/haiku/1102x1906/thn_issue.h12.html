New Year’s dawn
the dog waits beside
his empty bowl

Jack Barry

 

deep winter
the thin bones
of her wrist

Patrick Sweeney

 

bone chilling —
not enough night
for all the stars

Gary Hotham

 

cool March breeze —
between faded tea towels
her frayed pink bra

Ignatius Fay

 

nascent spring
this warm loaf
of ten-grain bread

Fay Aoyagi

 
 
 

white walls
white pills . . .
another day

Edith Bartholomeusz

 

winter night
the sound of mother’s
sewing machine

Dietmar Tauchner

 

winter evening
a young woman smiles
from the hair dye box

Joseph M. Kusmiss

 

first spring day —
even out of doors
I want to go out

Christopher Patchel

 

daffodil shoots . . .
underneath a bandage
the cut begins to itch

Bill Kenney
