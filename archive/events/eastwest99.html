For examples of renku see the Yuki Teikei renku page. Other web sites with renku are listed on the Yuki Teikei links page. The first link of a renku, the hokku, is similar to a haiku. In fact, the first collection of haiku ever written was actually a collection of hokku, compiled by Matsuo Basho (1644 - 1694).

Renku and before that renga, have honored positions in Japanese literary history. These forms, along with today's new adaptations of linked verse, embody the spirit of collaboration. Contest Rules
  • Hokku must be three lines, and contain a seasonal reference to summer. The theme should be related to the idea of East meets West.
  • A list of summer season words that are commonly used to write a renku or haiku is available at the Yuki Teikei Haiku Society web site.
  • Verses should not be published nor under consideration for other contests or publications.
  • By submitting an entry the author agrees to allow the Yuki Teikei Haiku Society to post the entry on East – West contest web pages and also in contest print material for promotional purposes.
  • The winning hokku as well as honorable mentions may also appear in Yuki Teikei Haiku Society publications (including the web site).
  • After the contest and the results have been posted, all rights revert to the author.
  • Submissions should be sent by email to the renku guide, Christopher Herold at christopher@theheronsnest.com
  • You may send in as many entries as you wish.
