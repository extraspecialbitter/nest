     I've chosen verse #34. It's this one, by Joann Klontz:

farm workers bend and curve
over the seedlings

     This links beautifully to Patricia's verse, in several ways. Firstly from the vantage point of the tourist at Macchu Picchu, we can see the workers far below striving unpretentiously, in harmony with nature. It's a natural extension of the previous verse. Secondly, the curves of the workers' backs link to clouds and mountains,. And thirdly, the link is to Neruda himself, who championed the underdog, those very workers who strived so hard for so little. And this verse also functions beautifully in the other way that it should, by shifting, away from the dreamy carefree feeling of a wealthy traveler, to the nitty gritty, down-to-earth life of working people.
