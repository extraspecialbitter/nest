     A bumper crop!

     Aside from the hokku, this verse has received more entries than any other stanza, which has made my decision very difficult since so many are so good! You have been very sensitive to what I'm striving to accomplish: create a well-balanced, interesting poem that (as Steve Mason has put it) twists and turns. Thank you for your enthusiasm. If we are successful in our creation of East-West '99, certainly it is due mostly to the skill of you who are giving such careful consideration to what is evolving, and what to contribute (nearly 300 stanzas thus far).

     Since there were so many fine verses offered in this round, I'm not going to cough up any commentary. If I were to delve into the whys and wherefores for any one verse, I'd feel obligated to do so for all the others that tantalized me. So I just buckled down and chose what I feel will be best for the overall poem÷not just the visible, written poem, but the poem that is you, the poets who are breathing spirit into what was once just an empty space.

     Verse #32 is by Carolyne Rohrig. It is:

the proud rustle

     Verse number #33 is a three-liner that continues in the realm of non-season. No 3rd person pronouns please; 1st or 2nd person is fine. Remember to stay light and up-beat. The shift should not be radical from now on. And I'd like to keep the emphasis on nature in this verse, though human affairs can be interwoven if done so with reserve.
