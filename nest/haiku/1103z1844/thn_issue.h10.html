every spring
the same question . . .
returning bees

Victor Ortiz

 

sunrise
a shoot half out
of its seed skin

Natalia Rudychev

 

faint scent of lilacs
I wonder what he means
by we

Harriot West

 

paper cranes
the yellow birch unpleats
its leaves

Catherine J. S. Lee

 

beachcombing . . .
a wave picks up and drops
the sun’s reflection

David Rodrigues

 
 
 

lantern light
the river slips
through our fence

Barbara Snow

 

first buds —
the oriole sings
its love song

Merrill Ann Gonzales

 

from the secret place . . .
the mother dog
with her litter

K. Ramesh

 

sun-dappled sidewalk
shadows of new leaves play
on stains of the old

Doug Kutney

 

moonless night
foxfire where there was
a forest road

Brent Partridge
