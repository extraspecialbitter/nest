gulls cry
in a gun-metal sky —
the still water

Patricia Prime

 

circling her grave
a bitter wind
shifts the family

Mark Alan Osterhaus

 

the hedgehog
yet to hibernate . . .
rain-matted leaves

John Barlow

 

under her coat
my shirt
first snowflakes

John Kinory

 

almost winter —
the blind man’s dog
looks at me

Andrea Cecon

 
 
 

lamplit breath
floats along the shore
ghost stories

Emma Dalloway

 

pecans falling —
we wait for our grandson
to visit

Lynn Edge

 

church bell
the smell
of snow

Jose del Valle

 

the headstones tilt
at different angles
dustings of snow

Matthew Paul

 

flickering lights . . .
he notices my handwriting
on the presents from santa

Duro Jaiye
