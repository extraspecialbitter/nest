sudden chill
from across the lake
another loon

Billie Wilson

 

first autumn
without mother
the leaves

Og Aksnes

 

before dusk
that thin strip of light
under the clouds

Hilary Tann

 

fallen leaves —
my old dog
finds his way

George Dorsty

 

Thanksgiving —
holding a bite
through the prayer

Collin Barber

 
 
 

mountain stream
two stone bridges
joined by mist

Margarita Engle

 

last leaves fall
the warmth of his voice
deep in his letters

Andrea Grillo

 

voting day —
crows on a cedar bough
shift their weight

Susan Constable

 

first frost
the smell of dust
burning in the ducts

Harriot West

 

Christmas Eve
cracking walnuts for his son
and his dad

David Giacalone
